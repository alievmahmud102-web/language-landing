# Target audience

## Primary segment

**B2B — education centers (SMB)**

- Language schools (kids, teens, adults).
- IT and digital skills training providers.
- Exam preparation centers (EGE, international exams, professional certifications—whatever is common in your geography).

**Organization size**: small to medium—typically **1–30** staff, **1–3** decision-makers for buying software/marketing help.

## Buyer roles

| Role | Motivation | Risk |
|------|------------|------|
| **Owner / director** | Revenue stability, reputation, marketing ROI | Budget, fear of failed IT projects |
| **Academic lead** | Quality of teaching, scheduling | Less interested in CRM unless it reduces chaos |
| **Admin / sales** | Daily inquiries, callbacks, spreadsheets | Adoption—must be simple |

## Student / parent side (influencer, not payer)

- Parents comparing schools online; need **trust**, **clarity**, and **low friction** to leave a request.
- Students may influence trial attendance; landing copy often speaks to both.

## Buying triggers

- Seasonal intake peaks; new competitor nearby; current site “does nothing”; leads lost in messengers; no idea which ad works.

## Disqualifiers (early stage)

- Centers that only want a cheap static page with no follow-up discipline.
- Enterprises needing deep ERP-grade customization without budget—politely refer or phase.

## Ideal customer (summary)

A center that **already gets inquiries** (or buys traffic) but **lacks a system** to respond fast, attribute sources, and convert trials to paid seats.
