# Positioning

## One-line positioning

**We build the system that brings parents in and turns inquiries into enrollments—not just another website.**

## Category

- Not “web design studio” first.
- Not “Tilda site” first.
- Not “AmoCRM implementer” first.

You are an **education growth system** vendor: **acquisition surface + operations + analytics**.

## For whom

SMB **education centers** that run on inquiries and trials and suffer when leads are messy or slow.

## Proof pillars

1. **Speed to lead** — fewer lost conversations.
2. **Source clarity** — see what actually works.
3. **Operator simplicity** — staff can use it weekly without IT.

## vs alternatives

| Alternative | Limitation | Our angle |
|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Generic web studio | Delivers pages; weak on **pipeline** and **measurement** | End-to-end **funnel** ownership |
| Site builders (Tilda, etc.) | Great publishing; CRM/analytics often bolted on inconsistently | **Purpose-built** flow for centers |
| Standalone CRM (e.g. Amo) | Powerful but generic setup cost; still need a **converting** front door | **Integrated** UX and faster time-to-value for this niche |

## Messaging do / don’t

- **Do**: talk outcomes (response time, conversion, source mix), show dashboard snippets in sales.
- **Don’t**: oversell custom dev without scope; don’t claim rankings without SEO scope.

## Elevator (30 sec)

> Most centers don’t lose on teaching—they lose on **follow-up and visibility**. We install a simple system: a landing that actually asks for a callback, a CRM that tracks every parent, and a dashboard so you know which channel paid off. One stack, one partner.
