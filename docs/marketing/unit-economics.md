# Unit economics

> Working sheet—replace assumptions with **your** data as deals close.

## Key definitions

- **CAC** (Customer acquisition cost): sales + marketing spend to win **one** paying center / **one** project, depending how you count.
- **Gross margin per project**: revenue − direct costs (hosting pass-through, subcontractor, paid tools for that client).
- **Payback**: months to recover CAC from margin (if you sell retainers, include recurring margin).

## Targets (3–6 months)

- **Stable deals** in **$700–$2,000** range: define what “stable” means (e.g. 1+ per month).
- **3–5 case studies**: amortize acquisition cost via shorter sales cycles later.

## Channel notes

- **Referrals** often lowest CAC—build a simple referral ask into offboarding of successful projects.
- **Paid ads** for your own agency: only scale when landing + CRM for **your** pipeline is measured.

## Review cadence

- Monthly: update average deal size, hours, win rate, top source.
